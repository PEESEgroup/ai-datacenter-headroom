# SI experiment findings (2026-05-16)

- Canonical SI inventory spans 51 CSV source tables with 106,547 tabular rows across Fig. 1--5 source, audit and shared-demand tables.
- Mid-2035 county allocation totals 84.3 GW, with 58.6 GW (69.6%) in the seven analyzed ISO/RTO regions and 25.6 GW (30.4%) outside them.
- Mid-2035 demand remains top-heavy: 335 positive counties, 19 counties above 1 GW, and top-50 share 74.3%.
- State-level concentration remains material but is not single-state dominated: the top 10 states hold 70.8% of mid-2035 county demand.
- Baseline midcase first-negative annual years: CAISO: >2035, ERCOT: >2035, ISO-NE: >2035, MISO: 2028, NYISO: >2035, PJM: 2033, SPP: 2028.
- A +/-2 GW additive headroom stress test mainly changes timing for marginal systems; it does not remove the ordering that MISO/SPP are earlier than PJM under the baseline convention.
- Generator-pipeline screen confirms very low effective-over-active conversion in several large queues; MISO effective/active is 0.19%, PJM is 10.3%.
- Archetype robustness is descriptive rather than statistical: nearest-neighbor stability ranges from 0.60 to 1.00 across leave-one-variable-out tests.
- Midcase nuclear retention+restart offsets 2035 baseline shortfalls unevenly: MISO: 50.4%, PJM: 45.7%, SPP: 10.5%.
- With 4 h storage and storage power at 50% of PV capacity, minimum PV ratios for <=1% residual are: ERCOT: 0.00x, MISO: 1.10x, PJM: 0.50x, SPP: 0.40x.
- H-frame gas backstop at 50% capacity factor has nonzero midcase emissions only in deficit-facing systems: MISO: 8.4 MtCO2/yr, PJM: 8.5 MtCO2/yr, SPP: 3.2 MtCO2/yr.
- LMP screen covers 66 harmonized zones; per-ISO zone counts range from 4 to 18.
